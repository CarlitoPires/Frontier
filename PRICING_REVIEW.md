# FRONTIER — Strategic SaaS Pricing Review

*Senior SaaS architect / business-strategist perspective. This is product
strategy guidance, not professional financial advice — validate with live
A/B tests before committing.*

## Current proposal

| Tier | Price | Scope |
|------|-------|-------|
| Base City | R$ 49,90 / mo | System access + one Base City |
| Global Citizen | R$ 79,90 / mo | Two languages/cities in parallel |

## The core tension

Your two stated goals pull in opposite directions:

- **High-volume acquisition** wants a low, frictionless entry point.
- **"Quiet Luxury" / premium positioning** wants price to *signal* exclusivity.

R$ 49,90 sits in an awkward middle. For the Brazilian market it is **not cheap
enough to be an impulse subscribe** (Duolingo Super is roughly in the same band,
and free alternatives anchor expectations near zero), yet **not expensive enough
to read as luxury**. A premium brand that charges ~R$ 50 invites the question
"why isn't this free like the app everyone else uses?" rather than "this must be
serious." Right now the price contradicts the brand story.

## Recommendations

### 1. Keep the `,90` ending — but go further with charm + prestige framing
`R$ 49,90` is standard charm pricing and fine. But for the *premium* tier,
prestige pricing (round numbers) often signals quality better than charm
endings. Consider `R$ 89` rather than `R$ 79,90` for Global Citizen — rounder
numbers are processed as more "considered/luxury," charm numbers as "discount."

### 2. Widen the gap to make the upsell obvious (dynamic anchoring)
A R$ 30 gap (49,90 → 79,90) is a 60% jump for "a second city" — many users
won't see the value mid-funnel. Two options:

- **Anchor high, sell the middle.** Introduce a visible third tier you don't
  expect most to buy (e.g. *Diplomat* — all cities + priority AI, R$ 149/mo).
  Its only job is to make Global Citizen look like the reasonable choice. This
  is decoy/anchoring and reliably lifts mid-tier conversion.
- Present **annual** pricing first at the paywall (anchor on the larger number,
  then reveal the "per month" equivalent) — standard SaaS anchoring.

### 3. Charge annually — it fits the product better than monthly
FRONTIER is a **125-hours-per-city, months-long commitment**. Monthly billing
fights your own model: a user grinds the gate, churns for two months, comes
back. Lead with annual (e.g. *R$ 399/yr ≈ R$ 33/mo equivalent*) framed as
"a year of citizenship." This improves LTV, smooths cash flow, and matches the
"conquer, don't rent" philosophy far better than a monthly meter.

### 4. Re-think *what* Global Citizen unlocks
"Two cities at once" is a **niche** upgrade — most learners want to finish one
city, not split focus (and your Hard Gate philosophy actively discourages
splitting). That makes 79,90 a hard sell on its current value prop. Stronger
premium levers that protect the brand:

- Faster / unlimited AI conversation minutes ("Free Flight" open-mic time).
- The **certificate as a credential** (verifiable, shareable) — people pay for
  proof, not just practice.
- Advanced analytics (the radar history, reaction-time trends).

Bundle those into the premium tier; reserve "second city" as one feature among
several rather than the headline.

### 5. Protect "Quiet Luxury" at the paywall
- No countdown timers, no flashing "50% OFF" — that destroys premium perception.
  Use restraint: a single, calm annual recommendation.
- A **7-day immersive trial that ends inside the narrative** (you "land" but
  can't clear Customs without citizenship) converts better than a feature
  paywall and stays in-world.

## Bottom line

The current R$ 49,90 / R$ 79,90 pair is a **reasonable starting hypothesis but
not the optimized endpoint.** Concretely:

1. Lead with **annual** billing (best fit for a months-long product + LTV).
2. Add a **high decoy tier** to anchor and make the premium tier obvious.
3. Repackage premium around **credential + AI minutes + analytics**, not just
   "second city."
4. Consider a **rounder** premium price (R$ 89) for luxury signalling.

Then **A/B test** R$ 49,90 vs a higher entry (R$ 59–69) — premium positioning
usually tolerates a higher floor than founders expect, and a higher price can
*increase* perceived value and reduce support-heavy low-intent churn. Let the
data, not intuition, set the final floor.
